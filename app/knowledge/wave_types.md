# Quivrr Wave Type Guidance

Beach Break
Often mixed, shifting and softer unless conditions are strong. Most surfers need paddle power, speed generation and forgiveness. Hybrids, grovellers, fish and everyday shortboards are common recommendations.

Point Break
Usually cleaner and more organised. Surfers can use boards with better hold, drive and rail line. Everyday shortboards, performance fish, mid lengths and step ups may all fit depending on size.

Reef Break
Can be powerful, hollow or fast. Recommend more hold, confidence and control. Avoid overly flat grovellers unless the reef is soft.

Small Weak Waves
Prioritise speed, width, foam, flatter rocker and easy wave entry.

Clean Powerful Waves
Prioritise hold, rail control, rocker and confidence at speed.

Bigger Surf
Consider step ups once the user is dealing with genuine size and power.
