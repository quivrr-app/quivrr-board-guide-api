# Quivrr Board Category Knowledge

Performance Shortboard
Designed for advanced surfers, steeper waves, cleaner faces and committed turns. Usually narrower, thinner and lower volume. Not the first recommendation for surfers who mainly need paddle power or confidence.

Everyday Shortboard
A more forgiving shortboard for normal surf. Good for intermediate to advanced surfers who want performance, but still need paddle support in mixed conditions.

Hybrid Shortboard
A blend between a shortboard and a fuller outline board. Useful for intermediate surfers who want turning ability with better paddle power and speed in average surf.

Groveller
Designed for weak, soft or small surf. Usually wider, flatter and higher volume for its length. Good when the surfer wants to catch more waves and keep speed in 1 to 4 foot conditions.

Fish
Fast, wide, skatey and good for softer waves. Suits surfers who want speed, flow and a looser feel. Not ideal when the surfer wants vertical performance or lots of hold in steep waves.

Performance Fish
A more refined fish style board. Good for intermediate to advanced surfers who want speed and flow but still want some control through turns.

Mid Length
Good for paddle power, trim, flow and earlier wave entry. Useful for beginner to intermediate surfers, older surfers, bigger surfers, or anyone wanting more glide without going to a longboard.

Funboard
Stable, forgiving and easy to paddle. Good for progression, confidence and small to medium surf. Often better than a shortboard for early stage surfers.

Mini Mal
Very stable and easy to paddle. Good for beginners, cruisy surfers and small wave conditions.

Step Up
Designed for bigger, stronger or more powerful waves. More length, hold and confidence at speed. Not for weak everyday surf.

Gun
For serious large waves only. Rarely recommended unless the user clearly surfs large, powerful waves.
